Brownie


Argentinian brownies often have a rich, fudgy texture and can be made with a variety of ingredients, including nuts, dulce de leche, or even a touch of espresso for extra depth. Here's a classic recipe for an Argentinian-style brownie with a fudgy texture, a touch of dulce de leche and with italian meringue.

Ingredients

Brownie

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups (400g) granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (140g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (85g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (175g) chocolate chips or chunks (optional)

Dulce de leche

  • 1/2 cup (150g) dulce de leche

Meringue

  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) water
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

How to make it

  1. Prepare the brownie batter

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13 inch (23x33 cm) baking pan or line it with parchment paper.
    • Melt the butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Remove from heat and stir in granulated sugar and brown sugar until combined.
    • Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
    • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips or chunks if using.
    • Pour the brownie batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly.

  2. Bake the brownie

    • Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs. Be careful not to overbake.
    • Allow the brownies to cool slightly while you prepare the Italian meringue.

  3. Make the Italian meringue

    • Combine the granulated sugar and water in a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves. Increase the heat and bring the mixture to a boil. Cook until the syrup reaches 240°F (115°C) on a candy thermometer.
    • Meanwhile, place the egg whites and cream of tartar in the bowl of a stand mixer (or use a hand mixer). Beat on medium speed until soft peaks form.
    • Once the sugar syrup reaches the correct temperature, slowly pour it into the whipped egg whites while the mixer is running on medium speed. Continue to beat until the meringue is thick, glossy, and cool to the touch. Add the vanilla extract and mix to combine.

  4. Top the brownies

    • Fill a pastry bag with dulce de leche and from the center, make a spiral out just before the edges.
    • To cover the edges, just go from the edge to the inner side, creating a flowery effect.
    • Spread the Italian meringue over the slightly cooled brownies, creating peaks and swirls as desired.
    • Use a kitchen torch to lightly brown the meringue, if desired. If you don’t have a torch, you can place the brownies under a broiler for a few seconds—watch closely to avoid burning.

  5. Cool and serve

    • Allow the meringue-topped brownies to cool completely before cutting into squares.

Serve and enjoy your brownie with dulce de leche and italian meringue!
