Chocotorta


Chocotorta is a delicious and easy-to-make Argentinian dessert that combines chocolate cookies, and a dulce de leche filling. Here’s a simple recipe to make your own chocotorta:

Ingredients

Filling

  • 2 cups (500g) of dulce de leche (you can substitute with caramel sauce if you can’t find dulce de leche)
  • 2 cups (500g) of cream cheese (or 1 cup if you prefer a firmer filling)

Cake

  • 1 package (about 400g) of chocolate cookies (Chocolinas are traditional, but you can use any chocolate cookies)
  • Coffee or milk for soaking the cookies (optional, but helps to soften them)

How to make it

  1. Prepare the filling

    • In a large bowl, mix the dulce de leche and cream cheese until smooth and well combined.

  2. Assemble the Chocotorta

    • If you’re using coffee or milk, quickly dip each cookie into the liquid (you don’t want them to get too soggy, just lightly coated).
    • In a serving dish or cake pan (a 9x13 inch pan works well), start by spreading a layer of the filling mixture.
    • Arrange a layer of cookies on top of the filling. You can place them side by side or overlap them slightly.
    • Spread another layer of the filling mixture over the cookies.
    • Continue layering cookies and filling until you use up the cookies and filling, finishing with a layer of filling on top.

  3. Chill

    • Refrigerate the chocotorta for at least 4 hours, but overnight is best to let the flavors meld and the dessert to set properly.

  4. Optional Topping

    • For an extra touch, you can top the chocotorta with grated chocolate, cocoa powder, or a drizzle of melted chocolate before serving.

Serve chilled, and enjoy your homemade chocotorta!
