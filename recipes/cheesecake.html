Cheesecake


Argentinian cheesecake, also known as "cheesecake" or "tarta de queso," is a delicious and creamy dessert that is slightly different from its American counterpart

Ingredients

Crust

  • 200 grams (about 7 oz) of digestive biscuits or graham crackers
  • 100 grams (about 3.5 oz) of unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar (optional)

Filling

  • 500 grams (about 18 oz) of cream cheese, softened
  • 200 grams (about 7 oz) of sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • 150 grams (about 5.5 oz) of granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice (optional)

How to make it

  1. Prepare the crust

    • Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F).
    • Place the digestive biscuits or graham crackers in a food processor and pulse until they turn into fine crumbs. Alternatively, you can put them in a zip-top bag and crush them with a rolling pin.
    • In a bowl, combine the crumbs with the melted butter and sugar (if using). Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand.
    • Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of a springform pan (about 9 inches in diameter). You can use the back of a spoon or the bottom of a glass to pack it down firmly.
    • Bake for 8-10 minutes, then remove from the oven and let it cool while you prepare the filling.

  2. Prepare the filling

    • In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth and creamy. You can use an electric mixer or a stand mixer for this.
    • Gradually add the granulated sugar, mixing well after each addition.
    • Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
    • Mix in the vanilla extract, flour, and sour cream or Greek yogurt. If you’re using lemon juice, add it here and mix well.
    • Pour the cream cheese mixture over the cooled crust in the springform pan.

  3. Bake

    • Bake the cheesecake for 45-50 minutes, or until the center is set but still slightly wobbly.
    • Turn off the oven, crack the oven door slightly, and let the cheesecake cool inside for about an hour. This helps prevent cracking.
    • After it has cooled, refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.

  4. Serve

    • Carefully remove the cheesecake from the springform pan.
    • You can top it with fruit, chocolate, or caramel sauce if you like, but it’s also delicious on its own.

Serve and enjoy your cheesecake!
